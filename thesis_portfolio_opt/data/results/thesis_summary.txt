
================================================================================
THESIS RESULTS SUMMARY
Macroeconomic Factor-Based Dynamic Portfolio Optimization
================================================================================

DATA:
  - 12 ETFs spanning equities, bonds, alternatives (2005-2024)
  - 18 FRED macroeconomic indicators
  - 212 engineered features (returns, vol, momentum, RSI, macro lags)
  - Strict temporal split: train ≤ 2021, test 2022-2024

MODELS TESTED:
  - 7 model types: Ridge, Lasso, ElasticNet, Random Forest, GBR, XGBoost, LightGBM
  - 84 model fits (7 models × 12 assets)
  - Time-series cross-validation (expanding window, 5 folds)
  - Walk-forward retraining (quarterly, 12 retrains)

KEY FINDINGS:

  1. PREDICTIVE POWER (OOS 2022-2024):
     - Best IC: +0.188 (Ridge) — macro factors have real forecasting power
     - Best directional accuracy: 50.9% (Ridge)
     - Gold and bonds most predictable (IC +0.24 to +0.37)
     - SPY hardest to predict (IC near zero) — supports EMH for large-cap

  2. PORTFOLIO PERFORMANCE:
     - Best fixed-split: Sharpe 0.803 (LightGBM macro, maxW=0.5) — beats SPY
     - Walk-forward (realistic): Sharpe 0.474 — beats Equal Weight and 60/40
     - Multi-agent swarm: Sharpe 0.313 — lower but adds risk insights
     - 22 strategies tested via autonomous experimentation

  3. METHODOLOGICAL CONTRIBUTIONS:
     - AutoResearch pattern (Karpathy): 12 batch experiments in 70 seconds
     - MiroFish adaptation: 9-agent swarm with agreement-based risk overlay
     - Walk-forward validation shows ~40% Sharpe degradation from fixed split
     - This gap quantifies overfitting in financial ML

  4. KEY THESIS ARGUMENTS:
     - Macro factors contain predictive information for safe-haven assets
     - Linear regularized models generalize better than tree models OOS
     - Multi-agent ensemble adds uncertainty quantification, not alpha
     - Walk-forward is essential — fixed splits overstate performance
     - LightGBM + macro-only features = best risk-adjusted strategy
