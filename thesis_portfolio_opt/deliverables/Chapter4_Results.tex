\section{Headline Result}
Walk-forward RandomForest with macro-only features achieves Sharpe ratio 0.969 out-of-sample (2022--2024), beating SPY buy-and-hold (0.671) with half the maximum drawdown ($-$12.3\% vs $-$22.1\%).

\section{Key Findings}
\begin{enumerate}
\item RandomForest + macro features achieves Sharpe 0.969 walk-forward (beats SPY)
\item Maximum drawdown of $-$12.3\% is half of SPY's $-$22.1\%
\item Top predictive features: 10Y Treasury yield, VIX, 2Y yield, BBB corporate spread
\item Transaction cost break-even: $\sim$25 bps (viable at institutional costs 5--15 bps)
\item OOS Sharpe difference is \textbf{not} statistically significant ($p = 0.79$) due to short sample
\item Strategy works during macro regime shifts (2022--2024), not in bull markets
\item Fama-French 5-factor $R^2 = 0.38$; 62\% of returns unexplained by standard factors
\item Ensemble (Sharpe 0.710) beats SPY but single RF is better
\item Turnover constraint at 10\% improves max drawdown from $-$22.6\% to $-$15.7\%
\end{enumerate}

\section{Statistical Significance}
Ledoit-Wolf test for Sharpe ratio equality (OOS only): $z = 0.27$, $p = 0.79$. The 3-year OOS window is too short to establish statistical significance at conventional levels. This is an honest limitation that motivates longer evaluation periods in future work.
